Computer Vision

Computer vision enables machines to locate, identify, and count objects in images and videos. At QSC, we build solutions that apply deep learning and image analysis to automate workflows, gain insights from visual data, and support research and operations in fields from conservation to manufacturing.

Wildlife Camera Traps

We automate the analysis of motion-triggered wildlife imagery, helping scientists monitor species, behavior, and ecological patterns efficiently with AI-based identification and tracking.

Drone Image Analysis

Our models extract meaning from aerial imagery — mapping land cover, detecting objects, and assisting with environmental monitoring, infrastructure inspection, and agriculture.

Fluid Sample Imaging

We use computer vision to detect and quantify particles in fluid samples — such as microplastics, algae, turbidity, or contaminants — supporting water quality monitoring and biomedical applications.

Scientific Contributions

Our team has contributed to peer-reviewed research in computer vision:

  • Machine learning to classify animal species in camera trap images
  • Automated classification of bat echolocation call recordings
  • Identification of animals in camera trap images: MLWIC2
  • Evaluation of platforms for AI processing of camera-trap data
