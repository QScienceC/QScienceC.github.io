Who we support

Typical outcomes

Engagement model

Start with a simulation-backed pilot, validate performance, then deploy decision policies into production workflows.

Case Study: Managing Production Schedules Under Changing Conditions

A manufacturer producing specialized components struggled to maintain efficient production schedules as orders, machine availability, and processing times changed throughout the day. Static scheduling rules worked under stable conditions but often broke down when disruptions occurred.

QSC developed a reinforcement learning-based scheduling framework that learned adaptive policies from simulated production environments. Instead of producing a single static schedule, the system continuously evaluated scheduling decisions as production conditions evolved.

The result was a more responsive scheduling process that helped planners prioritize jobs, allocate machine time, and manage disruptions more effectively.

What is Reinforcement Learning?

RL is best for high-variance environments where teams must balance throughput, quality, and risk under changing conditions.

  • Agents are trained to explore and act in dynamic environments
  • Feedback is used to improve future decisions
  • Goals are achieved by maximizing long-term rewards
